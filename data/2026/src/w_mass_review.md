# W boson mass — PDG 2026 review (preview)

The 2026 edition of the PDG *Review of Particle Physics* is being deployed
chapter-by-chapter ahead of the consolidated database release. As of 2026-05-15
the **W mass review** (review #54) and the **Standard Model / Electroweak
review** (review #10) are already accessible at `pdg.lbl.gov/2026/reviews/`
(the directory listing is hidden but the files are served). The Particle
Listings, the `mass_width` file, and the `pdgall` SQLite database are not yet
deployed for 2026.

- Source: PDG *Review of Particle Physics* 2026, review "Mass and Width of the
  W Boson" (Section 54).
- Downloaded from https://pdg.lbl.gov/2026/reviews/rpp2026-rev-w-mass.pdf
  on 2026-05-15. Review dated **1 December 2025**.
- The PDF is copyrighted and **not** stored in this repository; only the
  extracted numeric values and the quoted lines below are kept.

## Headline

**m_W = 80.36922 ± 0.0133 GeV** (world average, unchanged from PDG 2025).
The world average is the LHC-TeV MW Working Group 2023 combination
(arXiv:2308.09417), which **excludes the CDF-II 2022 result** because including
it gives only a 0.5% probability of compatibility across all measurements.

## Individual measurements (Figure 54.1)

| experiment | m_W (GeV) | in world avg? |
|---|---:|---|
| LEP-2 combined | 80.376 ± 0.033 | yes |
| D0 (Tevatron) | 80.383 ± 0.023 | yes |
| ATLAS (Run 1, 2017) | 80.370 ± 0.019 | yes |
| LHCb (2022) | 80.354 ± 0.032 | yes |
| **World Avg** | **80.36922 ± 0.0133** | (χ²/dof = 0.5/3) |
| CDF II (2022) | 80.4335 ± 0.0094 | **no — outlier** |
| ATLAS 2024 (refined Run 1) | 80.3665 ± 0.0159 | not yet — combination ongoing |
| CMS 2025 (Run 2) | 80.3602 ± 0.0099 | not yet — combination ongoing |

## W width

Γ_W = 2.14 ± 0.05 GeV (error scaled by 1.7).

## Standard Model prediction

From the electroweak fit (Section 10), excluding the direct m_W input:
m_W = 80.356 ± 0.006 GeV, Γ_W = 2.090 ± 0.001 GeV.
